\subsubsection*{template$<$typename T$>$\newline
class MODEL\+::\+Blood\+Vessel\+Junction$<$ T $>$}
\mbox{\hyperlink{class_m_o_d_e_l_1_1_blood_vessel_junction}{Blood\+Vessel\+Junction}}. 

Models a junction with one inlet and arbitrary outlets using \mbox{\hyperlink{class_m_o_d_e_l_1_1_blood_vessel}{MODEL\+::\+Blood\+Vessel}} elements between each inlet and outlet pair.

The governing equations are mainly defined by the blood vessel elements (see \mbox{\hyperlink{class_m_o_d_e_l_1_1_blood_vessel}{MODEL\+::\+Blood\+Vessel}} for more information). For the sake of brevity, the blood vessel contributions are not repeated here. In addition to the individual blood vessel contributions, the following equation ensures mass conservation between the inlets of the blood vessel elements\+:

\[
Q_{in}=\sum_{i}^{n_{outlets}} Q_{in, i}
\]\hypertarget{class_m_o_d_e_l_1_1_blood_vessel_junction_autotoc_md18}{}\doxyparagraph{Local contributions}\label{class_m_o_d_e_l_1_1_blood_vessel_junction_autotoc_md18}
\[
\mathbf{y}^{e}=\left[\begin{array}{llllllllll}P_{in, 1}^{e} & Q_{in, 1}^{e}
& P_{out, 1}^{e} & Q_{out, 1}^{e} &
\dots & P_{out, i}^{e} & Q_{out, i}^{e} & Q_{in,1} & \dots &
Q_{in,i}\end{array}\right] \]

Mass conservation

\[
\mathbf{F}^{e} = \left[\begin{array}{llllllllll}
0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 & 0 & -1 & \dots & -1 \\

\multicolumn{10}{c}{\mathrm{< blood \, vessel \, contributions >}} \\

\end{array}\right]
